Spell Thief

A weaver of stolen magic, a parasite in the arcane ecosystem.

Spell Thieves do not only study magic — they steal it. They siphon spells mid-cast, hijack curses meant for allies, and redirect devastating effects back at their enemies. Reactive, tactical, and dangerous to spellcasters, a Spell Thief turns arcane power into a liability.

Class Features

As a Spell Thief, you gain the following class features.

Hit Points

  • Hit Dice: 1d8 per Spell Thief level
  • Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier

Proficiencies

  • Armor: Light armor, medium armor
  • Weapons: Simple weapons, rapiers, shortswords, scimitars, hand crossbows, whips, bows
  • Tools: Thieves' tools
  • Saving Throws: Dexterity, Intelligence
  • Skills: Choose three from Acrobatics, Arcana, Deception, Insight, Investigation, Perception, Sleight of Hand, Stealth

Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) A rapier or (b) a shortsword
  • (a) A hand crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) a simple weapon
  • (a) Studded leather armor or (b) Chain shirt
  • Thieves' tools
  • An arcane focus (dagger, ring, glove, tattoo, or similar object)

Larceny Dice

You gain Larceny Dice, which fuel your spell-stealing abilities.

  • Number of Dice: You have a number of Larceny Dice equal to your proficiency bonus
  • Die Size: Your Larceny Dice are d6s initially
  • Recovery: You regain all expended Larceny Dice when you finish a short or long rest

Spellcasting

2nd-level Spell Thief feature

You are an arcane half-caster who learns magic through theft and observation.

  • Spell Slots: You use the half-caster progression. See the Spell Thief table for your total spell slots.
  • Spellcasting Ability: Intelligence
  • Spell Save DC: 8 + proficiency bonus + Intelligence modifier
  • Spell Attack Modifier: Proficiency bonus + Intelligence modifier
  • Preparing Spells: You prepare spells from your spellbook after a long rest. You can prepare a number detailed in the class table.
  • Spellbook: You begin with a spellbook containing three 1st-level spells from the Spell Thief base list.

Learning Spells

  • Stolen Spells: When you successfully steal a spell using Arcane Pickpocket, you can attempt to learn it. Make an Intelligence (Arcana) check (DC = 10 + spell level). On a success, you transcribe the spell into your spellbook over the next 8 hours per spell level (minimum 8 hours), costing 100 gp per spell level. Only spells on the wizard spell list can be transcribed this way.
  • Wizard Spells: You can learn any wizard spell from a scroll or spellbook. This takes 32 hours per spell level (minimum 32 hours) and costs 200 gp per spell level.

Core Features

Arcane Pickpocket

1st-level Spell Thief feature

When a creature you can see within 60 feet casts a spell, you may use your reaction and expend 1 Larceny Die to attempt to steal it.

Make an Intelligence (Arcana) check. DC = Enemy Spell DC + the spell's level (minimum 1).

You can attempt to steal any spell, but you have disadvantage on checks to steal spells of a level higher than half your Spell Thief level (rounded up).

On a Success

Flip a coin. Before the coin lands, ask the GM to call heads or tails.

  • If the GM calls it correctly: the original spell fails and has no effect, though the caster's spell slot is still expended.
  • If the GM calls it incorrectly: the original spell still occurs, but you have successfully copied it.

The spell is stored in your mind until the end of your next turn (only one spell can be stored at a time). You may cast it using your action, bonus action (if applicable), or reaction without expending a spell slot, using your own spell save DC and spell attack modifier.

On a Critical Theft (Natural 20): You can slightly modify the stolen spell (GM approval).

On a Failure

Roll the Larceny Die and reduce any damage you take from that spell by the amount rolled. You cannot steal the spell.

On a Critical Mishap (Natural 1): The DM chooses one:

  • The spell is cast on you in addition to the original target
  • The spell's effects are duplicated, if already targeting you
  • You take 1d6 psychic damage per spell level as magical feedback

Cunning Action

1st-level Spell Thief feature

You can take a bonus action on each of your turns in combat to take the Dash, Disengage, or Hide action.

Redirect Magic

2nd-level Spell Thief feature

When you or a creature within 60 feet takes damage from a spell you can see, you may use your reaction and expend 1 Larceny Die to redirect the magical energy.

  • Roll the Larceny Die and add your Intelligence modifier
  • Reduce the damage by that amount
  • You may redirect the reduced damage to a different creature you can see within 60 feet of the original target
  • The redirected damage is the same type as the original spell

Fighting Style

2nd-level Spell Thief feature

You gain a Fighting Style feat of your choice (except Blind Fighting or Great Weapon Fighting).

Larcenous Discipline

3rd-level Spell Thief feature

You choose a specialized path: Arcane Reaver, Hex Broker, or Mirror Savant. Your choice grants features at 3rd, 6th, 10th, and 14th level.

Ability Score Improvement

4th-level Spell Thief feature

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify. You gain this feature again at levels 8, 12, and 16.

Extra Attack

5th-level Spell Thief feature

You can attack twice instead of once whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

Spell Interception

5th-level Spell Thief feature

Concentration Theft

You can steal spells that require concentration. On a success:

  • You may immediately begin concentrating on it
  • The original caster automatically loses concentration
  • You can attempt Arcane Pickpocket as a bonus action instead of a reaction

Arcane Sense

You can cast detect magic at will, without expending a spell slot. Additionally, you learn whether creatures you can see are spellcasters and the highest-level spell slot they have available.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1), regaining all uses on a long rest.

Focus Specialization

7th-level Spell Thief feature

Choose one specialization: Status Transposition or Arcane Absorption.

Additionally, as a bonus action, you can expend a spell slot to gain a number of Larceny Dice equal to the slot's level (cannot exceed your normal maximum).

Status Transposition

As a bonus action, expend 1 Larceny Die to steal and manipulate magical effects within 60 feet. Choose one:

  • Transfer: Move a magical condition or buff from one creature to another within range
  • Suppress: End one magical condition on a creature (roll the die as a bonus to any saving throw needed)
  • Amplify: Extend the duration of a magical condition by 60 seconds

Uses equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1), regaining on a long rest.

Arcane Absorption

When you take damage from a spell, as a reaction (without expending a Larceny Die), you can:

  • Absorb: Gain temporary hit points equal to half the spell damage taken (rounded down), applied after damage if you are not reduced to 0 hp
  • Convert: Change the damage type of the spell to a type of your choice before it affects you

Uses equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1), regaining on a long rest.

Improved Arcane Larceny

11th-level Spell Thief feature

  • You can store up to two stolen spells simultaneously
  • Stored spells last for 1 minute instead of until the end of your next turn
  • You gain advantage on Arcane Pickpocket checks against spells of 3rd level or lower
  • When you fail an Arcane Pickpocket check, roll the Larceny Die twice and take the higher result when reducing damage

Grand Theft Magic

15th-level Spell Thief feature

Once per long rest, when a creature you can see within 60 feet casts a spell of 4th level or lower, you may use your reaction to automatically steal it with no check required.

  • The spell fails completely and the spell slot is expended
  • You immediately store the spell (does not count against your Spell Cache limit)
  • The spell lasts for 10 minutes instead of 1 minute
  • You may cast it at any time within that duration without expending a spell slot

Master Larcenist

17th-level Spell Thief feature

  • You regain 1 Larceny Die when you roll initiative if you have none remaining
  • When casting the stolen spell, roll a d20 — on an 18 or higher, the spell is not forgotten and can be used again
  • You can use Arcane Pickpocket 2 times per round
  • When you steal a spell, you can immediately cast it as part of the same reaction/bonus action

Elusive

18th-level Spell Thief feature

No attack roll has advantage against you while you aren't incapacitated.

Epic Boon

19th-level Spell Thief feature

You gain an Epic Boon feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

Arcane Heist

20th-level Spell Thief feature

Once per long rest, activate Arcane Heist as a bonus action for 1 minute. While active:

  • You can use Arcane Pickpocket and Redirect Magic without expending Larceny Dice
  • You can steal multiple spells per round (up to your number of reactions)
  • You gain additional reactions equal to your Intelligence modifier (only for Arcane Pickpocket or Redirect Magic)
  • You automatically succeed on concentration checks to maintain stolen spells
  • You can steal and maintain concentration on two spells simultaneously

Larcenous Disciplines

At 3rd level, you choose a discipline that defines your approach to magical theft. Your choice grants features at 3rd, 6th, 10th, and 14th level.

Arcane Reaver

A frontline anti-mage who punishes spellcasters with blade and stolen sorcery.

Spellblade Strike

3rd-level Arcane Reaver feature

When you successfully steal a spell, you can immediately make one melee weapon attack as part of the same reaction, dealing an additional 1d6 force damage. This increases to 2d6 at 10th level and 3d6 at 14th level.

Arcane Reflex

3rd-level Arcane Reaver feature

You gain one additional reaction per round usable only for Arcane Pickpocket or Redirect Magic. Additionally, when an attacker you can see hits you with an attack, you can use your reaction to halve the attack's damage against you.

Antimagic Strikes

6th-level Arcane Reaver feature

When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can roll a Larceny Die to add its value as damage and force the target to make a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed save, choose one:

  • The creature loses concentration on one spell
  • The creature has disadvantage on its next spell attack roll
  • The creature's spell save DC is reduced by your Intelligence modifier until the end of your next turn

You can cast one of your cantrips in place of one of your attacks.

Combat Theft

10th-level Arcane Reaver feature

When you use Extra Attack, you can replace one attack with an Arcane Pickpocket attempt (without using your reaction). You can now use Spellblade Strike and Antimagic Strikes with small ranged or throwable weapons. Additionally, when a creature within 40 feet casts a spell, you can make an opportunity attack against them.

Counterspelling Flurry

14th-level Arcane Reaver feature

You can now use Spellblade Strike and Antimagic Strikes with all weapons and gain a second extra attack. When you steal a spell, until the end of your next turn:

  • Your movement speed increases by 15 feet
  • You have advantage on weapon attack rolls
  • Your spells gain an additional 20 feet of range
  • You can re-roll the steal spell coin flip

Hex Broker

A master manipulator of curses, hexes, and debilitating conditions.

Curse Collector

3rd-level Hex Broker feature

You gain the Status Transposition option from Focus Specialization (you do not choose a Focus Specialization at 7th level). When you steal a debuff or harmful magical condition, you gain temporary hit points equal to 1d6 + your Intelligence modifier.

You learn the hex spell. It doesn't count against your spells known, and you can cast it once without expending a spell slot (regained on a short or long rest). You can learn stolen spells from the warlock spell list.

Hex Weaving

3rd-level Hex Broker feature

When you use Status Transposition to transfer a harmful condition, you can spend an additional Larceny Die to transfer it to two creatures instead of one. Both targets make saving throws as normal.

Invert Fortune

6th-level Hex Broker feature

When you steal a beneficial magical effect (such as bless, haste, or invisibility), you can invert it into its negative counterpart when transferring it (e.g., bless → bane, haste → slow, invisibility → faerie fire). The inverted effect uses your spell save DC and lasts for the same duration as the original spell.

Condition Stacking

10th-level Hex Broker feature

When you transfer a magical condition to a creature already suffering from a different magical condition, both effects are enhanced:

  • Saving throws against both conditions: disadvantage for enemies, advantage for allies
  • Damage from both effects increased by 1d6
  • Duration of both effects extended by 1 round

You can maintain this enhancement on a number of creatures equal to your Intelligence modifier.

Plague Broker

14th-level Hex Broker feature

As an action, expend 1 Larceny Die to create a 20-foot-radius sphere of contagious magic centered on a point within 60 feet. All creatures must make an Intelligence saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed save, each creature shares the magical conditions currently affecting any creature you can see. Allies gain beneficial conditions; enemies share harmful effects. Each lasts for 1 minute.

Affected creatures can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of their turns. Once used, you can't use this feature again until you finish a long rest.

Mirror Savant

A trickster who reflects, duplicates, and misdirects magical energy.

Reflective Theft

3rd-level Mirror Savant feature

When you successfully steal a targeted spell (one that targets specific creatures), you can immediately redirect it to a different valid target within the spell's range. The new target must make the original saving throw or be subjected to the spell attack roll, using your spell save DC or spell attack modifier.

Illusory Decoy

3rd-level Mirror Savant feature

When you steal a spell, you leave behind a magical duplicate of yourself (as per mirror image) that lasts until the start of your next turn. The duplicate:

  • Appears in your space and moves with you, mimicking your actions
  • Is indistinguishable from you until destroyed
  • Causes attackers to roll a d20 to determine whether the attack targets the duplicate instead (as per mirror image)
  • Has AC equal to 10 + your Dexterity modifier; destroyed if hit
  • Grants advantage on your next Stealth check while it exists

Spell Echo

6th-level Mirror Savant feature

When you cast a stolen spell, you can expend a Larceny Die to create a weaker echo. Roll the Larceny Die: on a 5 or higher, you can cast the spell again immediately at a different target at base spell level, using your spell save DC and attack modifier. You can't create an echo of an echo.

Swap Fate

10th-level Mirror Savant feature

When a spell targets you or an ally within 30 feet, you can use your reaction and expend a Larceny Die to swap positions with that creature. The spell now targets you instead, and:

  • You appear in the ally's space; the ally appears in yours
  • You can immediately attempt to steal the spell using Arcane Pickpocket as part of the same reaction
  • The swapped ally gains the benefits of your Illusory Decoy feature (a duplicate lasts until the start of your next turn)

Mirror Cascade

14th-level Mirror Savant feature

Once per long rest, when you steal a spell of 5th level or lower, you can create four echoes of that spell instead of one. Each echo can target a different creature or area within the spell's range. The echoes all resolve simultaneously, use your spell save DC and spell attack modifier, and are cast at base spell level.

Multiclassing

Minimum Ability Scores: Intelligence 13, Dexterity 13

Proficiencies Gained: Light armor, simple weapons, thieves' tools

Design Philosophy

The Spell Thief is designed as a reactive half-caster comparable to Paladins and Rangers in power level.

  • Limited Resources: With 2–6 Larceny Dice and half-caster spell slots, Spell Thieves must choose their moments carefully.
  • Reactive Nature: Many features require reactions, meaning Spell Thieves sacrifice opportunity attacks and some defensive options.
  • Scaling DCs: As enemies cast higher-level spells, Arcane Pickpocket becomes harder without Improved Arcane Larceny's advantage.
  • Martial Parity: Extra Attack, Fighting Style, and medium armor keep Spell Thieves competitive when magic isn't being thrown around.

DM Guidance

  • Spell Identification: Allow Intelligence (Arcana) checks as a free action, or simply inform the player what spell is being cast.
  • Legendary Resistance: Can be used to prevent spell theft, as with any other effect.
  • Reaction Timing: Arcane Pickpocket must be declared when the spell is cast, before effects resolve.
  • Concentration Limits: Spell Thieves can only concentrate on one spell at a time until 20th level.
  • Multiclass Interactions: A Spell Thief multiclassed with a full caster can become very powerful — monitor carefully.
